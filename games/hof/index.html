The Idea: If the decoder already has a dictionary of all possible words, the encoder only needs to output index numbers instead of full words. "pāṭimokkha" (12 chars) becomes just [47]. This is how LLM tokenizers work — but here we prove it with a simple word-level dictionary.

⚠️ Why the savings are modest: Common words like "de", "is", "van" are already 1 token in Claude's BPE tokenizer. Your index [3] takes ~1-2 tokens to output. So you save on rare/long words (pāṭimokkha: 12 chars → 1 index) but gain nothing on short common words. Net savings: ~20-30% at best for natural text. For highly repetitive text, savings are much higher.
