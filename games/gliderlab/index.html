Glider Lab — interactive companion to “Glider Surgery”

A 7→1 four-state hexagonal cellular automaton. Each rule is a 16,384-entry table. This page runs the actual CA, the fractal rule generator, and the direction-law math in your browser — no server, no libraries. Steer a glider, see why it works.

The direction law: a glider’s heading = angle(F) + 180°, where F = Σ ap·dp is the single-neighbour activation vector — for each of the six hex directions, “does an isolated upstream cell there switch the centre on?” (just 18 of the 16,384 entries).

Steer a glider by editing 18 numbers

Click the compass to request a heading. The app rewrites only the 18 single-neighbour entries so that F points the right way, then runs the glider. The white arrow is the law’s prediction angle(F)+180°; the orange trail is where the glider actually goes. They agree.

Route a glider with a steering field

Tile space with rules that differ only in those 18 entries, so each column steers a slightly different way (heading rises 0°→ the slider value across the board). The glider keeps its shape but follows the field — a programmable waveguide. The background tint shows the local target heading.

Curving the field bends the glider; sharp jumps can scatter it — gentle gradients route best (matches the cluster sweep: best recipes use a ~0.85-board gradient).
