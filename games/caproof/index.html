🧬 Cellular Automaton "Compression" — Proof of Concept

Encoding text into a CA initial state, running it, and comparing output vs. expected.
Demonstrates why this is harder than it sounds.

💡 The Idea: Instead of outputting translated text directly (limited by LLM output tokens), output a compact CA rule + initial state that *generates* the text when executed.
⚠️ The Problem: Finding the right initial state is an inverse problem — and tiny errors cascade into total corruption. Watch below.

🎓 Takeaway: Even with a simple 1D 2-state CA (Rule 90/110), just 5% noise in the initial state causes massive corruption after a few generations. A 4-color 2D CA would be exponentially harder to control. The inverse problem — "what initial state produces my exact Dutch text?" — is computationally intractable for an LLM to solve in a single shot. Beautiful theory, brutal practice.
