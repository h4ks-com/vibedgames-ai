History of Peano Numbering

Peano numbering is a way of representing natural numbers using a set of axioms proposed by the Italian mathematician Giuseppe Peano in 1889. Peano's axioms define the natural numbers (0, 1, 2, 3, ...) through a set of formal rules. The key axioms are:

  • 0 is a natural number.
  • Every natural number has a successor, which is also a natural number.
  • 0 is not the successor of any natural number.
  • Different numbers have different successors.
  • If a property is possessed by 0 and also by the successor of every number that has that property, then it is possessed by all natural numbers (principle of induction).

Peano's work significantly contributed to the foundational aspects of mathematics and the formalization of number theory.
