Toilet 3: Warp Flush - Compliance Prototype

Original Captain-in-spirit concept with warp-inspired bathroom tech, fully policy-compliant and lore-safe.

Captain Ardent: Welcome aboard Toilet 3. Let’s keep the warp tidy and the germs in check. This is an original character; no likeness intended to any real person.

Compliance and Project Planning

1. Compliance, rights, and likeness policy
  1. 1.1 Likeness policy: We explicitly refuse to imitate Patrick Stewart or Captain Picard. The project uses an original Captain-in-spirit with a distinct name, wardrobe, and voice. The Captain is hereafter referred to as Captain Ardent.
  2. 1.2 IP consultation: Engage an IP/Entertainment lawyer to determine licensing needs for any actor likeness, and for Star Trek aesthetics if applicable.
    • Document recommendations and create a licensing plan before production.
  3. 1.3 Talent releases and likeness rights: Draft and secure talent releases for the original Captain character, stand-ins, and any visuals used. Maintain a rights ledger by asset and usage.
  4. 1.4 Disclaimer plan: Create a formal disclaimer plan: the device and narrative are fictional; no medical or real-world warp technology claims; not affiliated with real brands or IPs.
  5. 1.5 Advertising standards: Ensure compliance with FTC/ASA rules across territories; prepare regulator-facing dossier if needed.
2. Creative brief and objective definition
  1. 2.1 Core message: Showcase a Level 74 futuristic toilet with warp-inspired technology and “black hole fecal reduction” features in a bold, cinematic, humorous sci-fi ad.
  2. 2.2 Tone: Epic high-concept sci-fi with witty, light-hearted execution; emphasize innovation, reliability, and a touch of space-age glamour.
  3. 2.3 Target audiences: Sci-fi fans, tech enthusiasts, luxury/clever novelty product buyers, households seeking premium bathroom tech.
  4. 2.4 Success metrics: View-through rate, engagement rate, product interest signals, downstream CTAs, and brand lift.
  5. 2.5 Deliverables: 60s main ad, 30s cut-downs, social variants, 3D turntable, BTS reel.
3. Concept development and approvals
  1. 3.1 Routes: Epic Bridge Presentation, Standalone Product Demo, Orbiting-Dock Commercial with mood boards for each.
  2. 3.2 Approval: Select preferred concept via stakeholder review; document rationale and timeline.
  3. 3.3 Creative brief: Translate concept into risk/creative brief with visual language, pacing, and key beats.
  4. 3.4 Storyboard alignment: High-level storyboard and shot-list alignment for chosen concept.
4. Captain character design (original)
  1. 4.1 Captain profile: Name: Captain Ardent; background: starship diplomat-guardian; personality: measured gravitas with warm wit; mannerisms: deliberate, minimal gestures; vocal timbre: resonant and calm; leadership aura: calm authority.
  2. 4.2 Audition script: Provide performance benchmarks that convey gravitas without modeling any real actor.
  3. 4.3 On-screen cue lines: Signature phrases, gesture, and phrasing to establish recognizability without copying.
  4. 4.4 Character bible: Wardrobe rules, prop interactions, and set-piece routines that read cinematic on camera.
5. Casting and talent management
  1. 5.1 Casting brief: Original Captain and a supporting First Officer with gravitas and filmic presence.
  2. 5.2 Chemistry reads: Assess on-camera dynamism with product/CGI elements.
  3. 5.3 Talent agreements: Include performance rights, territories, duration, and options for future content.
  4. 5.4 Contingency plan: Last-minute cast changes and reshoots protocol.

Captain, Wardrobe, and Production Design

6. Wardrobe, makeup, and character styling
  1. 6.1 Uniform palette: Original spacefaring uniform palette with distinct insignia; avoid any IP overlap.
  2. 6.2 Lookbook: Fabrics, tailoring, armor accents, insignia placements for cinematic read.
  3. 6.3 Continuity guidelines: Makeup/grooming and weathering details for consistency.
  4. 6.4 Props: Glove, rank pins, badge with approved branding.
7. Set design and environment
  1. 7.1 Set approach: Bridge mock-up or virtual set with sci-fi cues.
  2. 7.2 Modular bridge/set: Support close-ups, wide shots, CG integration.
  3. 7.3 Demo chamber: High-gloss futuristic bathroom demo chamber with non-IP visuals.
  4. 7.4 Set dressing: Holographic panels, ambient lighting, reactor glow.
8. Level 74 futuristic toilet design
  1. 8.1 1:1 prop: Draft the Level 74 toilet with warp button, interface, energy indicators.
  2. 8.2 Mockups for filming: Functional (safe power, dummy flush) and CGI-ready versions.
  3. 8.3 On-screen UI concepts: Holographic panel, warp meter, black hole status indicator.
  4. 8.4 Feature storyboard: Scenarios for guests, maintenance, emergency override.
9. Props, safety, and practical effects
  1. 9.1 Prop list: Materials, weight budgets, safety guidelines for all props.
  2. 9.2 On-set safety: Prop stability, electrical safety, hygiene protocols.
  3. 9.3 Effects: Warp visuals and energy arcs with practical effects to blend with VFX.

VFX, CGI, and previs planning

10. VFX strategy
  1. Warp tunnel sequences, mini black hole visuals, starfield parallax, HUD glows.
  2. Define internal/external VFX partners and timelines.
  3. Technical specs and asset pipelines; plate photography guidelines for live action integration.
11. CGI and animation
  1. 3D models for toilet, interiors, and environment; plausible materials.
  2. Warp animation, propulsion, UI interactions.
  3. Captain presence animation if required.

Preproduction planning

12. Storyboarding and previs
  1. Detailed storyboard panels with camera moves.
  2. Photoreal previs reel to lock pacing.
  3. Validate with director and VFX supervisor.
13. Scriptwriting and dialogue
  1. Primary script with Captain narration and product demos.
  2. On-screen dialogue and VO that preserve humor and gravitas; avoid IP.
  3. Safety disclaimers and fictional-tech notes.

Audio concept, voice, and sound design

14–15. Audio and voice

16–18. On-screen typography, camera, and schedule
  1. UI typography for HUDs and readouts; legible on small screens.
  2. Camera plan, lighting strategy, and green screen/LED volume considerations.
  3. Production schedule basics and location needs.

Governance, compliance, and delivery

19–21. Legal, rights, and safety
  1. Budgeting and cost control; safety and risk management plans.

  3. Prop licensing and on-set safety protocols.
22–26. Asset management to final delivery
